Difficulty of Prediction

"Chi entra papa in conclave ne esce cardinale." — This Italian proverb ("He who enters the conclave as pope exits as cardinal") reflects the historical unpredictability of papal elections. Early favorites often fail to secure the papacy.

For example, during the 2013 conclave, bookmakers did not list Jorge Mario Bergoglio (later Pope Francis) among the top six most likely candidates (O'Connell, 2019). His election was seen as a surprise to many external commentators.

However, retrospective social network analyses suggest a different story. Five years after his election, an exploratory analysis of episcopal consecration networks showed Bergoglio occupying a strong hidden position in the global Catholic network — probably already before his papal election (even though historic reconstructions remain difficult).

Social network theory emphasizes that "actors who are better connected or occupy more strategic positions often have access to greater resources, information, and influence" (Borgatti & Everett, 2006). Thus, internal network dynamics may decisively influence leadership selection.

Construction of Network Indicators

This section describes the indicators used to analyze the cardinal networks during the 2025 Conclave.

Conclave 2025 Network Indicators

  • Number of ties – The number of direct consecration links to or from a cardinal within the conclave network.
  • Betweenness – How often a cardinal appears on the shortest paths between other cardinals.
  • PageRank – Influence based on connections with other influential cardinals.

All Bishops Network Indicators

  • Number of ties – Total consecration links in the global bishops network.
  • Betweenness – Cardinal's strategic position across the worldwide episcopal network.
  • PageRank – Influence within the broader global Catholic hierarchy.

Other Indicators

  • Betting probability – Chance of election derived from public betting markets, sourced from OddsChecker (April 29, 2025).
  • Reputation – Number of awards received according to Wikidata entries.
  • Expert opinion – Number of expert mentions across major media sources: Detroit Catholic, The Independent, ABC News, Wired Italy, Il Giornale, BBC News, CBS News, and Domradio.

Correlation Matrix

Key Findings

  • Network centrality measures like ties, betweenness, and PageRank are highly interrelated. This reflects a tightly connected conclave network.
  • Betting probability and reputation moderately correlate with centrality, suggesting that more central figures enjoy greater visibility and expectations.
  • Pagerank among all bishops and number of ties among all bishops are nearly interchangeable in capturing global influence.

Interpretation

Cardinals with more connections occupy more central network positions, which leads to higher reputation and perceived electability.

While most network measures overlap strongly, Pagerank captures slightly distinct aspects of influence compared to simple tie counts or betweenness.
